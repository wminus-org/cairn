# Cairn — Positioning

The pitch reference. Three lines, one shift, seven verticals, one structure. Look it up here instead of improvising it on stage.

Derived from [`PLAN.md`](../PLAN.md) § Positioning and § The shift. The three lines below are verbatim from the plan — say them as written, they have been tuned.

---

## The three lines

### Consumer line

> Cairn lets you leave your voice somewhere, so it's only heard by whoever stands there next.

**When:** the opening. First fifteen seconds, before any screen is explained.
**To whom:** the whole room. This is the line that makes people lean in, and it needs no setup.

### Team line

> Cairn pins your team's knowledge to the physical thing it's about. Stand in front of the valve, hear everything anyone has ever said about that valve.

**When:** the turn — after the consumer cairn has played and landed, as you switch into Space mode.
**To whom:** the operators and the builders in the room. It reframes the same demo they just watched as a work tool without changing a single thing on screen.

### The line for the VCs

> Every company with people in the field has institutional memory that only exists as photos in a group chat. We attach it to the location instead, and it unlocks when you're there.

**When:** the last forty seconds, after "Brief me" has played out loud.
**To whom:** the investors — Silicon Gardens, Bek, anyone doing diligence math while you talk.

### Quick table

| Line | Moment | Job it does |
|---|---|---|
| Consumer | Open, 0:00–0:15 | Emotional hook. Buys attention for everything after. |
| Team | The turn, mid-demo | Converts charm into utility. Same primitive, new frame. |
| VC | Close, last 0:40 | Names the market and the wedge. Only place the B2B vocabulary appears. |

## The shift: v1 → v2

v1 was an art project — leave a voice note at a bench. Charming, hard to pitch to a room containing Silicon Gardens and Bek.

v2 keeps the poetry and adds the wedge. **The primitive did not change:** information pinned to a physical location, unlocked by being there. What changed is who has that problem and how expensive it is for them.

If someone asks "did you pivot?" — no. Nothing in the build changed. A cairn is a cairn whether the stone is a love note or a fault report. The consumer version is the demo; the field version is the business.

## Verticals — and what the pain looks like today

Say these words **only in the last forty seconds**. Before that they flatten the demo.

| Vertical | What it looks like right now |
|---|---|
| **Construction handovers** | The sub who ran the conduit finishes and leaves. What's behind that wall exists in his phone and his head, and the next trade drills into it. |
| **Facilities maintenance** | The same air handling unit has been fixed four times by three contractors. Every visit starts by rediscovering what the last one already worked out. |
| **Hotel and property inspections** | The snag list is 200 rows of "Room 412, bathroom, staining." Nobody can tell which stain, so somebody walks the floor a second time to find out. |
| **Retail merchandising audits** | Head office ships a planogram, the store sends photos back into a group chat, and no one can match a photo to the fixture it was taken of. |
| **Film location scouting** | The scout shoots 400 frames and writes notes in a doc. Six weeks later the director asks where the power drop was, and the answer is in nobody's memory. |
| **Agricultural plots** | The agronomist's observation about the north corner of block 7 is a voice message in a thread. Unfindable by the following season. |
| **Event build-outs** | Last year's crew knew where the floor boxes were and where the ceiling drops. This year it's a different crew, and they find out by hitting it. |

The common shape: **someone stood in front of the thing, knew something true about it, and had no way to leave it on the thing.**

## The WhatsApp framing

This is the sentence that does the most work with a non-technical room, because everyone has already lived it:

> Institutional memory lives in WhatsApp photos.

Unpack it only if asked, and only this far:

- A photo in a chat has **no anchor**. It shows a valve, but not *which* valve, on which floor, in which building.
- It has **no addressability**. You cannot ask a group chat "what do we know about this door."
- It **expires with the person**. When the guy who took it leaves, the thread is archaeology.
- The alternative on offer today is a PDF or a ticketing system nobody opens standing on a ladder.

Cairn's answer in one clause: **we attach it to the location instead.** The location is the index. Being there is the query.

## Pitch structure

Consumer demo, B2B business. Do not invert this.

| Beat | Content | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Consumer line + the entrance cairn | It lands emotionally in fifteen seconds. Let the audio play. Do not talk over it. |
| 2 | The photo-pin cairn | Show the real physical object on camera while the pins are on screen. This is where "charming" becomes "useful." |
| 3 | **The turn** — team line | Switch to Space mode. Same map, same gesture, different stakes. |
| 4 | "Brief me" on the eleven-stone cairn | Let the phone talk to the room. Silence while a machine summarizes three months of a place's history is the moment that wins this. |
| 5 | The judges' cairn, then the VC line | Close standing on it. They can only hear it because they are there. |

Rules that hold for all five beats:

- **Walk while pitching.** Mirror the phone to the screen. The proximity mechanic is invisible if you stand still.
- **Do not narrate architecture.** No one is buying the server-side gate. If a judge asks how the gate works, that's a question, not a slide — answer in one sentence and move.
- The words *construction, facilities, field service, property* appear **once**, in beat 5. Earlier they turn a beautiful demo into a sales deck.
